About me
======

I'm a PhD student at the [Department of Statistics and Data Science](https://statistics.wharton.upenn.edu/), Wharton School. 

I am fascinated by the success of perturbation genomics, with CRISPR technology as the leading example. In modern genomics, statistical methods must address **calibration, power, and computation jointly** — weaknesses on any one axis undermine the value of the others. My research advances all three fronts:

- **Calibration and robustness.** Perturbation-based testing — resampling, permutation, and subsampling — for sharp Type-I error control under low signal-to-noise ratio and complex null dependence. **Representative works:** [permuted score test](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2501.03530), [symcrt](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.14698).
- **Power.** Compound power analysis along two tracks: practical platforms for experimental design, and theory through the compound decision framework and nonparametric techniques. **Representative works:** [PerturbPlan](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.05.22.727199v1.full.pdf).
- **Computation.** Mathematical theory drawing on classical asymptotic analysis to keep methods scalable and provably reliable. **Representative works:** [spaCRT](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2407.08911v4).

In addition to research, I'm passionate about teaching and mentoring since I am deeply grateful for and benefit from the supervisors I have met, who have taught me far beyond just statistics. I have been a mentor for [Undergraduate Research in Probability and Statistics](https://sites.google.com/view/urps-penn?pli=1) and [Directed Reading Program](https://www2.math.upenn.edu/~tbraz/drp/) at UPenn.
